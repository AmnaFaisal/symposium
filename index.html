International Postgraduate Computer Science Research Symposium 13th & 14th November 2024

Highlights

School of Computer Science at Taylor’s University invites our esteemed academic partners to the International Postgraduate Computer Science Research Symposium (IPCRS), a platform for postgraduate research students to showcase their research work to a diverse audience, including both academic and industry professionals. The 2024 symposium will be held on the 13th and 14th of November in a hybrid format at Taylor's University Lakeside Campus and online, offering flexibility for all participants.
Highlights of this year’s symposium include:

  • Invited keynote talks by eminent academic and industry experts in cybersecurity, AI, and data science.
  • Presentations (oral and poster sessions) by postgraduate researchers on the latest innovations and findings.
  • Interactive forums designed to foster collaborations between academia and industry, providing valuable insights of the current and future research trends and challenges.
  • Opportunities for networking and building partnerships across academic and industry sectors.
  • Engaging discussions on the future of technology and its impact on society.

We look forward to celebrating the innovative work of postgraduate students and we do hope that you can join us for this exciting event.

Keynote Speakers

Keynote Speaker

I am a Reader at the School of Computer Science & Informatics at Cardiff University. I led the “Supervisory Control and Data Acquisition Systems Cyber Security Lifecycle (SCADA-CSL)” project, funded by Airbus Endeavr Wales, where I developed a SCADA Cyber Security, Safety, and Risk (CSSR) graphical extension for BPMN 2.0 and a SCADA system dependency model. In 2020-2021, I led a project funded by NCSC and RISCS on cybersecurity decision-making by SMEs. I was awarded an EPSRC grant in 2021 to develop a framework for cybersecurity playbooks enhancing CNI resilience. I also lead cyber skills education and am passionate about equality and diversity in cybersecurity.

Keynote Speaker

Dr. Fathi Amsaad (Senior Member, IEEE) is an Assistant Professor in the Department of Computer Science and Engineering, with a joint appointment in Biomedical, Industrial, and Human Factors Engineering at Wright State University, Dayton, OH. He earned his Ph.D. in Engineering, specializing in Computer Science and Engineering, from the University of Toledo in 2017. Dr. Amsaad directs the SMART Cybersecurity Research Lab at Joshi Research Center. His research focuses on Cyber and Physical System Security, specializing in Hardware-Oriented Security, including cryptographic hardware, counterfeit detection, secure SoC design, and side-channel attack countermeasures.

Keynote Speaker

Professor Dr. David Asirvatham is the Executive Dean of the Faculty of Innovation and Technology at Taylor’s University, Malaysia. With over 30 years of experience in lecturing and managing ICT projects, his research interests include digital neural networks, e-learning technologies, big data analytics, and AI. He has held numerous leadership roles, including Director of the Centre for Information Technology at the University of Malaya. Dr. Asirvatham has a Ph.D. from Multimedia University and an M.Sc. from Brunel University, UK.

Keynote Speaker

Professor Dr. Noor Zaman Jhanjhi is a distinguished Computer Science Professor at Taylor’s University, Malaysia. He serves as the Program Director for Postgraduate Research. Recognized among the world’s top 2% scientists in 2022 and 2023, he has over 700 research publications and numerous international patents. Dr. Jhanjhi has received multiple accolades, including the Vice Chancellor’s Award, the Most Cited Researcher award at Taylor’s University, and the Outstanding Researcher Award. His expertise spans Cybersecurity, IoT security, and Software Engineering.

Keynote Speaker

Professor Dr. Sayan Kumar Ray is the Head of the School of Computer Science at Taylor’s University, Malaysia. With a Ph.D. in Computer Science from the University of Canterbury, New Zealand, his research focuses on 5G/6G networks, IoT applications in disaster management and healthcare, and dynamic spectrum sharing. Dr. Ray has over 2,000 citations and numerous publications. He has held various academic leadership roles, including Chair of the Manukau and Tech Park Campus Research Committee in New Zealand.
